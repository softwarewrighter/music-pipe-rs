Bach Toccata - Full Organ Arrangement

Abstract
The iconic opening of Bach's Toccata and Fugue in D minor (BWV 565), arranged for multi-voice church organ. Features the famous dramatic descending runs played in octaves across four MIDI channels, with deep pedal bass providing the thunderous low D that makes this piece instantly recognizable in horror films and classical concerts alike.
Script Details
examples/demo-bach.sh # Pipeline stages used: seq --notes "D5/2*127 C#5/32*120 D5/32*120 A4/32*115..." --bpm 66 --ch 0 --patch 19 seq --notes "D4/2*127 C#4/32*120 D4/32*120 A3/32*115..." --bpm 0 --ch 1 --patch 19 seq --notes "D3/1*127 A3/1*127 D4/1*127 F4/1*127..." --bpm 0 --ch 2 --patch 19 seq --notes "D2/1*127 A1/1*127 D2/1*127" --bpm 0 --ch 3 --patch 19 cat upper.jsonl lower.jsonl chord.jsonl pedal.jsonl | viz | to-midi
Explanation
  • 4 channels for upper octave, lower octave, chord, and pedal bass
  • Patch 19 = Church Organ (MIDI General MIDI standard)
  • 32nd notes (/32) for the rapid mordent and descending runs
  • Octave doubling creates the massive organ sound (D5+D4 simultaneously)
  • Velocity dynamics (*127, *120, *115) for musical expression
  • 66 BPM tempo for dramatic, weighty phrasing

Bach Toccata - 8-bit Arcade Style

Abstract
A chiptune-inspired rendition of the Toccata opening, reminiscent of the 1983 arcade game Gyruss which famously used an electronic arrangement of this Bach masterpiece. Single channel, square wave synthesis, and a punchy 140 BPM tempo capture the energy of classic arcade gaming.
Script Details
examples/demo-bach-arcade.sh # Pipeline stages used: seq --notes "D5/4*127 C#5/32*120 D5/32*120 A4/32*115 G4/32*110 F4/32*110 \ E4/32*105 D4/32*105 C#4/32*110 D4/8*120 R/8 A4/4*125 ..." \ --bpm 140 --ch 0 --patch 80 --vel 120 \ | viz \ | to-midi
Explanation
  • Single channel monophonic melody (classic chiptune constraint)
  • Patch 80 = Square Lead (closest to 8-bit square wave synthesis)
  • 140 BPM fast arcade tempo for energy and excitement
  • Three phrases: D figure, A figure, return to D with low finish
  • No harmony - pure melody line as in early game audio chips
  • Inspired by Gyruss (1983) by Konami

Bach-esque - Algorithmic Background Music

Abstract
Procedurally generated baroque-style organ music using the motif stage's seed-driven algorithm. Three voices (upper flourishes, middle counterpoint, pedal bass) are generated independently and combined, creating original music inspired by Bach's style rather than transcribing his notes directly. Suitable for ambient background listening.
Script Details
examples/demo-bach-esque.sh SEED=1685 # Bach's birth year # Upper manual - dramatic flourishes seed $SEED | motif --base 62 --notes 48 --complexity 9 --bpm 132 \ --ch 0 --vel 100 --patch 19 --dur 0.5 --rest-prob 0.1 \ | scale --root D --mode minor # Pedal bass - sustained tones seed $((SEED+1)) | motif --base 38 --notes 12 --complexity 2 \ --ch 1 --vel 90 --patch 19 --dur 2.0 \ | scale --root D --mode minor # Middle voice - counterpoint seed $((SEED+2)) | motif --base 50 --notes 24 --complexity 6 \ --ch 2 --vel 85 --patch 19 --dur 0.75 --chord-prob 0.15 \ | scale --root D --mode minor cat upper.jsonl bass.jsonl mid.jsonl | viz | to-midi
Explanation
  • Seed 1685 (Bach's birth year) for deterministic generation
  • motif stage generates melodic patterns algorithmically
  • scale stage quantizes notes to D minor scale
  • Complexity 9 for upper voice = more melodic variation
  • Complexity 2 for bass = simple, stable foundation
  • dur 2.0 for bass = half notes (slow, sustained)
  • chord-prob 0.15 = occasional chords in middle voice
  • Each voice uses a different seed offset for variety
